    About F.A.L.C.O.N

    Overview

    Firefighting robots represent a critical advancement in safety technology, designed to navigate hazardous environments and reduce fire risks without endangering human personnel. Our team is developing a robot that can detect, approach, and help extinguish fires using sensors, artificial intelligence, and autonomous navigation. This system is especially useful in dangerous settings such as industrial areas, factories, chemical laboratories, and other confined spaces where emergencies can become life-threatening.

    Mission

    We believe that improving firefighter safety is essential in every emergency response. Our mission is to create a robot that assists in fire suppression within hazardous and high-risk environments, helping reduce direct exposure to danger. By supporting firefighting operations through smart and reliable technology, we aim to enhance efficiency, protect lives, and contribute to safer and more effective emergency response systems.

    Team

    We are a dedicated group of students, developers, and innovators working together to design and build a functional firefighting robot. Each member contributes skills in research, programming, electronics, design, and problem-solving. United by a shared goal, our team collaborates to transform ideas into a practical solution that can help protect lives during fire emergencies.

    How It Works

    F.A.L.C.O.N follows a smart automated process to detect and extinguish fires efficiently.

    1

    Flame Detection and Response Priority

  • When F.A.L.C.O.N. detects a flame, it prioritizes moving toward it over patrolling and stops at a safe distance of 11–20 cm from the fire source.

    • 2

    Fire Extinguishing Mechanism

  • Once in position, the robot activates its fire suppression system using an MG995 servo (rotating 18°–27°) to trigger and direct the extinguisher spray toward the flame while maintaining a safe operating distance.

    • 3

    Power System

  • The F.A.L.C.O.N. uses two separate 18650 Li-Ion battery packs, with a 2-cell pack powering sensors and control systems and a 3-cell pack powering the motors, and is fully rechargeable with the extinguisher placed for easy refilling.

    • 4

    Dual-Control System (Master–Slave Architecture)

  • The Master system processes sensor data (flame detection and ultrasonic obstacle avoidance) and controls the servo for fire response, while the Slave system handles all movement functions and operates only under Master instructions.

    • 5

    Dual System Behavior

  • The robot operates with parallel roles where the Master handles sensing and decision-making while the Slave executes movement commands, and the Slave cannot function independently without Master input.

    • 6

    Manual Override Mode

  • The system allows manual override by connecting directly to both Master and Slave systems, enabling independent or combined control while still allowing the Master to automatically trigger fire suppression when a flame is detected.
